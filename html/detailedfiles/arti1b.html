ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

Semester II - Detailed Syllabus

Unit Topics
Unit 4: Foundation of AI & Search as Optimization
4.1: History of AI: Alan Turing and cracking Enigma, Mark I Machines, 1956 – The Birth of the Term AI, AI Winter of the 1970s, Expert Systems of the 1980s, Journey to Present-Day AI. Distinction between AI, Pattern Recognition, and Machine Learning.

4.2: Search as Optimization: How to Search for the Best Answer to a Question (e.g., Playing Tic-Tac-Toe).
• State Space Search – Different States as Different Solutions
• Mathematical Equation for Optimizing a Result with Tic-Tac-Toe Example
• Expanding Possible States and Choosing the Best State
Uninformed Search: Breadth-First Search, Depth-First Search
Informed Search: Heuristic Search (Tic-Tac-Toe Example), Greedy Best-First Search, A* Search (Basic Idea), Hill Climbing (Basic Idea with Example), Simulated Annealing (Basic Idea Only)

4.3: Evolution and Darwin's Theory, Inspiration for Evolutionary Algorithms, Concepts of Crossover and Mutation, Random Selection (Russian Roulette), Optimization Using Genetic Algorithms.
• Survival of the Fittest
• Solution Vector as Genes (Binary String Example)
• Crossover and Mutation on Binary Strings
• Fitness Function
• Practical Example: Finding Root of a Univariate Equation (Include Libraries and Problem)
Unit 5: Knowledge Representation and Reasoning
Logic in Computer Science, Propositional Logic, Logical Expressions, Truth Tables, Conjunction, Disjunction, Syllogism, Tautology, De Morgan’s Theorem.
• Logical Propositions (Atomic & Compound)
• NOT, AND, OR as Negation, Conjunction, Disjunction
• Implication and Biconditional Statements
• Truth Table for Proof
• Commutativity, Associativity, Distributive Rules
• Practical Examples for Inference
• Simple Concepts: Unification, Clause, Inference (with Examples)
• Example: Answer Extraction System
• Brief Introduction to Fuzzy Logic (Basic Idea Only)
Unit 6: Uncertainty Management
Handling Uncertain Knowledge, Uncertainty and Rational Decision-Making, Probabilistic Reasoning, Bayes’ Rule, Conditional Probability.
• Probabilistic Inference Using Bayes’ Rule
• General Method (Simple Cases)
• Combining Evidence
Unit 7: Preliminary Concept of Chatbots
What is a Chatbot?
• Examples of Different Chatbots
• Flowchart Describing Basic Working Principle of Chatbots
