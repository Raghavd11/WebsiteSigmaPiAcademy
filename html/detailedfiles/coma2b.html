COMPUTER APPLICATION

Semester IV - Detailed Syllabus

Unit Topics
Unit 3: Database Management System
Introduction:
•Drawbacks of Legacy System, Advantages of DBMS, Layered Architecture of Database, Data Independence, Data Models, Schemas and Instances, Database Languages, Database Users, DBA, Data Dictionary.
Entity Relationship (ER) Modeling:
•Entity, Attributes and Relationship, Structural Constraints, Keys (Super Key, Key, Candidate Key, Alternate Key, Primary Key), ER Diagram of Example Databases, Weak and Strong Entity Sets, Specialization and Generalization, Their Constraints, Aggregation.
Relational Model:
•Basic Concepts of Relational Model, Relational Algebra.
Integrity Constraints:
•Domain Constraints, Referential Integrity, View.
SQL:
•Introduction, DDL and DML, Data types (char(n), varchar(n), int, float, date), Constraints (not null, unique, primary key), Commands: create/use/show/drop database, show tables, create/describe/alter/drop table, insert/delete/select/update.
•Operators: Mathematical, Relational, Logical. Clauses: aliasing, distinct, where, in, between, order by. Handling NULL: is null, is not null, like.
•Aggregate Functions: max, min, avg, sum, count. Grouping: group by, having clause.
•Joins: Cartesian product, equi-join, natural join.
Unit 4: Data Warehousing and Data Mining
Introduction:
•What is Data Warehouse? Data Warehouse Modelling, Data Cube and OLAP, Data Warehouse Implementation.
•Data Mining: What kind of data can be mined, what kind of patterns can be mined, Data Cleaning, Data Transformation Strategies, Overview.
Data Mining Applications and Trends:
•Mining Sequence Data, Time Series, Symbolic, Biological, Statistical Data Mining, Visual and Audio Data Mining, Applications and Trends in Data Mining.
Unit 5: Foundation of Artificial Intelligence (AI)
Introduction to Artificial Intelligence:
•Definition and Scope of AI, Historical Overview and Key Milestones, Differentiating AI from Human Intelligence.
AI Subfields and Technologies:
•Machine Learning: Supervised, Unsupervised, and Reinforcement Learning.
•Deep Learning and Neural Networks, Natural Language Processing (NLP), Computer Vision.
Applications of AI:
•AI in Finance: Fraud Detection, Algorithmic Trading, Risk Assessment.
•AI in Customer Service and Chatbots.
•AI in Education: Personalized Learning, Intelligent Tutoring Systems.
Ethical and Social Implications of AI:
•Bias and Fairness in AI Systems, Impact on Employment and Workforce, AI and Social Inequality.
