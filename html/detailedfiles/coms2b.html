COMPUTER SCIENCE

Semester IV - Detailed Syllabus

Unit Topics
Unit 1: Database Management System
1.1: Introduction to DBMS
• Drawbacks of Legacy System
• Advantages of DBMS
• Layered Architecture of Database
• Data Independence
• Data Models, Schemas and Instances
• Database Languages, Database Users, DBA, Data Dictionary

1.2: Entity Relationship (ER) Modelling
• Entity, Attributes and Relationship
• Structural Constraints
• Keys: Super Key, Key, Candidate Key, Alternate Key, Primary Key
• ER Diagrams for Example Databases
• Weak and Strong Entity Sets
• Specialization and Generalization
• Constraints on Specialization and Generalization
• Aggregation

1.3: Relational Model
• Basic Concepts
• Relational Algebra

1.4: Integrity Constraints
• Domain Constraints
• Referential Integrity
• View

1.5: Structured Query Language (SQL)
• Introduction to SQL: DDL and DML
• Data Types: char(n), varchar(n), int, float, date
• Constraints: not null, unique, primary key
• Commands: create database, use, show databases, drop database, show tables
• Table Handling: create, describe, alter (add/remove attributes, primary key), drop
• Data Manipulation: insert, delete, select, update
• Operators: mathematical, relational, logical
• Clauses: aliasing, distinct, where, in, between, order by, is null, is not null, like
• Aggregate Functions: max, min, avg, sum, count
• group by, having clause
• Joins: Cartesian product, equi-join, natural join
Unit 2: Foundation of Artificial Intelligence
2.1: Introduction to Artificial Intelligence
• Definition and scope of AI
• Historical overview and key milestones
• AI vs Human Intelligence

2.2: AI Subfields and Technologies
• Machine Learning: Supervised, Unsupervised, Reinforcement Learning
• Deep Learning and Neural Networks
• Natural Language Processing (NLP)
• Computer Vision

2.3: Search as Optimization
• Basic Concepts
• Strategies for State Space Search
• Data-Driven vs Goal-Driven Search
• Heuristic Search
• Breadth-First Search (BFS), Depth-First Search (DFS)
• A* Search

2.4: Applications of AI
• AI in Finance: Fraud detection, algorithmic trading, risk assessment
• AI in Customer Service: Chatbots
• AI in Education: Personalized learning, Intelligent Tutoring Systems

2.5: Ethical and Social Implications of AI
• Bias and fairness in AI
• Impact on employment and workforce
• AI and social inequality
